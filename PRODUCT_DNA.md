```
PRODUCT: Mizan — Islamic productivity companion (faith-anchored day planner)
JOB-TO-BE-DONE: when a Muslim wants to live a balanced, intentional day,
  they want prayers, dhikr, Quran, focus and learning in one calm place,
  so they can stay consistent without juggling many apps.
HERO USER: practicing Muslim, bilingual (AR/EN), values both deen and
  productivity; phone-first, novice-to-intermediate app sophistication.
DIFFERENTIATOR: the 5 daily prayers are the literal backbone of the planner —
  the day is organized AROUND salah, not productivity bolted onto a habit app.
PROOF: streaks, points, XP/badges, prayer + task completion history.
EMOTION IN→OUT: scattered / guilty about consistency → grounded, balanced, calm.
FEEL-WORDS x3: balanced, serene, precise.
VOICE 1–5: formal 2 · serious 3 · expert-friendly 3 · calm 4 (gentle, never naggy).
ANTI: must never feel gamey-loud, harsh, preachy, or like a generic SaaS
  dashboard; no aggressive red urgency; no stock-mosque clip-art.
PRICE FEEL: fair-premium (polished, but warm and accessible).
```

## DNA → design consequences
- emotion-out = calm → whitespace ↑, gentle springs, muted accent use, slow
  cinematic moments reserved for the hero scale.
- serious 3 / calm 4 → spring overshoot ≤2% on content, no confetti storms;
  celebration limited to genuine milestones (goal complete, streak).
- balance metaphor → the ميزان scale is the one signature mark; motion = a
  scale settling into equilibrium (settle, don't bounce).
- bilingual-native → Arabic is designed, not translated; full RTL; Cairo AR /
  Sora EN; Quran in Amiri/Naskh.

## Voice → copy
Headline = the promise in the user's words: "Balance in All Things" /
"التوازن في كل شيء". CTA = verb+object tied to calm ("Get Started" / "ابدأ الآن").
Errors stay gentle and recoverable.
